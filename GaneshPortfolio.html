Overview

What is Rapido?

Rapido is India's largest bike taxi platform, operating in 100+ cities with over 50 million registered users. It connects passengers with bike captains for quick, affordable last-mile commutes. Despite strong traction, the app suffered from friction-heavy booking flows, inconsistent UI patterns, and poor trust indicators that were hurting conversion and retention.

This is a self-initiated redesign case study exploring how thoughtful UX intervention could significantly improve the core booking experience.

Problem Space

What was broken.

After conducting competitive analysis against Ola, Uber, and Namma Yatri, and synthesizing user reviews from the Play Store and App Store, four core pain points emerged:

📍
Confusing Location Picker
Users struggled to set precise pickup points, often requiring 2–3 attempts to get accurate location before booking.
⏳
Opaque Wait Times
No real-time ETA feedback during captain assignment created anxiety and led to booking cancellations.
🧭
Poor Navigation Hierarchy
Bottom navigation was overloaded, making it hard for new users to discover features like scheduled rides and offers.
🔒
Weak Safety Signals
Safety features (SOS, live tracking share) were buried deep in the UI — a critical trust gap for solo riders.

Research & Insights

What users told me.

I conducted a heuristic evaluation of the existing app against Nielsen's 10 usability principles and reviewed 200+ user reviews across Play Store and Twitter to build a qualitative picture of friction points.

"I always worry the captain won't find me — the map doesn't show me where exactly I should stand. I just pick a random landmark and hope for the best."

I also created 2 primary user personas: the daily office commuter (age 22–30, time-sensitive) and the occasional user (age 18–45, safety-conscious). These personas guided every design decision throughout the project.

Design Decisions

How I solved it.

1. Smarter Location Input — Introduced a two-step location confirmation with a precision pin drop and nearby landmark suggestions. Users can now see a "captain's view" preview before confirming pickup.

2. Live Wait Indicator — Added a pulsing real-time ETA ring during captain search with distance-to-user updates every 30 seconds, reducing perceived wait anxiety.

3. Simplified Navigation — Collapsed the bottom tab bar from 5 to 3 items, moving secondary features into a progressive disclosure "More" drawer. Task completion improved in prototype testing.

4. Safety First Redesign — Elevated SOS and "Share Trip" to a persistent floating action button visible throughout the active ride, with one-tap access — no more digging through menus.

Reflections

What I learned.

This case study reinforced my belief that great UX is deeply contextual. Rapido's users aren't just booking a ride — they're navigating busy intersections in Hyderabad or Chennai with one hand on their bag. Every tap, every moment of confusion, has a real-world cost.

If I were to take this further, I'd conduct field research — riding with actual users during peak hours — and run quantitative A/B tests on the location confirmation flow, which I believe holds the highest conversion impact.
